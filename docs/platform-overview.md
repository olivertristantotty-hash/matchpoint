# MATCHPOINT — Platform Overview

## What It Is

MATCHPOINT is a peer-to-peer skill-based competition platform where players challenge each other to 1v1 video game matches with real stakes. Players wager tokens against each other, play their match, and the winner takes the pot. The platform operates through a Discord server (where matches are organized and played) and a companion website (where deposits, withdrawals, and account management happen).

## How It Works

### Two Modes

**Free Play** — Players receive 1,000 free virtual coins daily. These coins have no real-world value and cannot be withdrawn. Free Play exists so players can experience the platform, build their reputation score, and compete without financial risk.

**Real Wagers** — Players deposit real money via the website, which is converted to tokens (100 tokens = $1.00 USD). These tokens are used to wager against other players. Winners receive the combined pot minus a 10% platform fee. Tokens can be withdrawn back to real money through the website.

### Match Flow

1. **Challenge** — Player A challenges Player B to a match in a specific game (e.g., FIFA, Call of Duty, Valorant) for a set number of tokens. Player B receives a notification.

2. **Accept** — Player B accepts the challenge. Both players' tokens are locked in escrow — neither player can access them until the match is resolved.

3. **Rules** — A private thread is created for the two players. The game's standard rules are displayed. Players can agree on custom rules (e.g., specific map, match settings). Both players must confirm the rules before proceeding.

4. **Play** — Both players confirm they're ready, then play their match on their chosen platform (console or PC).

5. **Evidence** — After the match, both players are prompted to drop a screenshot of the final score screen into the thread. For PC players with Medal.tv installed, gameplay clips are captured automatically.

6. **Settlement** — Players can "Quick Settle" by both independently reporting the result (win or loss). Reports are blind — neither player sees what the other reported. If both reports agree, the winner is paid automatically. If reports conflict, a dispute is opened and a moderator reviews the screenshot evidence.

### Money Flow

- **Deposits**: Players sign into the website with their Discord account and deposit funds. Funds are converted to tokens at a fixed rate (100 tokens = $1.00 USD). In the future, deposits will be processed via USDC (a stablecoin pegged to the US dollar) on a low-cost blockchain network.

- **Escrow**: When a wager is accepted, both players' stakes are moved from their available balance to escrow. Escrowed tokens cannot be used for other wagers or withdrawn. This guarantees the winner can be paid.

- **Settlement**: When a match result is confirmed, the platform calculates the payout: total pot minus a 10% fee. The winner's available balance is credited. The loser's escrowed tokens are forfeited.

- **Withdrawals**: Players can withdraw their available token balance back to real money via the website. Withdrawals will be processed as USDC transfers to the player's wallet address.

- **Platform Revenue**: The platform earns revenue through the 10% fee on every settled real-money wager. Free Play wagers have no fee.

### Trust & Safety

- **Reputation System** — Every player has a reputation score starting at 100. Honest play increases reputation; no-shows and rule violations decrease it. Reputation determines the maximum wager amount a player can place, starting low and scaling up as trust is established.

- **Betting Limits** — New players can only wager small amounts. Higher stakes are unlocked as reputation grows. This means a bad actor can only cause minimal damage before being caught.

- **Zero Tolerance** — Submitting fake results or forged evidence results in an immediate permanent ban. All linked game accounts (Steam, Xbox, etc.) are blacklisted, preventing the banned user from returning with a new Discord account.

- **Verified Accounts** — Players must link at least one game platform account before placing real wagers. Steam and Xbox accounts are verified through a code-in-profile process. Linked accounts are tied to the player's identity — if banned, those accounts cannot be used on the platform again.

- **Screenshot Evidence** — Players are required to screenshot the final score screen after every match. In any dispute, the player without a screenshot loses. This creates a strong incentive for both players to capture evidence.

- **Blind Reporting** — Neither player sees what the other reported until both have submitted. This prevents one player from strategically matching or contradicting the other's report.

- **First Reporter Timeout** — If one player reports and the other doesn't respond within 15 minutes, the first reporter's result is automatically confirmed. This prevents sore losers from stalling.

- **Audit Trail** — Every action in a match (wager creation, rule confirmation, ready checks, match start/end, screenshots, reports, settlement) is timestamped and logged in the match thread. This provides a complete record for dispute resolution.

### Supported Games

The platform supports any game that can be played as a 1v1 or team match. At launch, the following games have preset rules and profiles:

- EA FC / FIFA
- League of Legends
- Valorant
- Rocket League
- Call of Duty
- Fortnite

Players can also wager on games in the "Other" category with custom rules agreed upon before the match.

### Legal Classification

MATCHPOINT facilitates contests of skill, not games of chance. The outcome of every match is determined by the players' skill in the chosen video game. This classification is consistent with how similar platforms (Players' Lounge, GamerSaloon, Checkmate Gaming) operate in the United States and United Kingdom. The platform does not offer, facilitate, or promote games of chance, lotteries, or casino-style gambling.
